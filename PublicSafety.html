Computing saves lives

Computing has been a monumental help in ensuring public safety.

Cybersecurity

We use technology every day, yet many of us forget the true dangers of being online. It is with thanks to firewalls, anti viruses and cryptograohy that our private information is not simply publically available to everyone at all times. You may roll your eyes every time the authenticator asks for two factor authentication and you have to whip out your phone for the 5th time, but this is exactly how we keep your banking details and money safe.

Emergency Services

The use of real time data provided by software such as Find My iPhone and enhanced communication systems have meant that response times and coordination responses of first responders have greatly improved. Furthermore, data analytics and machine learning algorithms have helped law enforcement agencies predict where crimes will occur based on historical data, meaning resources can be allocated more efficiently. This has also been used in healthcare, for example, during the COVID-19 pandemic, computing systems were used to track cases, predict outbreak hotspots, and manage resource distribution. Contact tracing apps also helped prevent the spread of the virus.

Natural Disaster Predictions

Computing allows for warnings of natural disasters. Countries such as Japan and Mexico use seisometer networks and real time data processing to detect primary waves, to warn for the more destructive p waves. This data is analyzed in real time and alerts people and automatically stops trains or shuts down infrastructure before damage can occur. Atmospheric and oceanic data can also be analyzed such as wind patterns, sea temperatures and pressure systems to predict hurrican and typhoon paths and intensities. Increasingly, machine learning algorithms are being applied to improve forecasting accuracy. There are many more uses of technology which include:

  • Flood prediction and management
  • Wildfire risk prediction
  • Volcano eruption forecasting
  • Tsunami prediction and detection
  • Tornado prediction
  • Drought forecasting and monitoring
  • Landslide prediction
  • Avalanche forecasting
  • Heatwave monitoring
  • Climate change models

Health Monitoring

Computing has meant that we are able to have continuous, real time tracking of vital signs almost constantly, allowing for early detection of medical issues, and personalised healthcare management. Through wearables, Internet of Things devices, and data analysis, health monitoring has become much more accessible, affordable, effective and preventative. Wearable health devices such as Fitbits and Apple Watches monitor vital signs such as heart rate and blood pressure. Alerts can then be received if there is abnormal activity. These devices can also monitor your sleep activity and are useful for helping diagnose and treat sleep disorders.

Smart Home Security

With the use of security cameras and motion sensors, we have all felt much safer in our own homes. Smart cameras now have the ability for AI powered video analytics, allowing humans, animals and other objects to be identified, inlcuding familiar faces. Systems such as Ring and Google Nest can identify package deliveries or suspicious activity outside of your homes. Remote monitoring and control has also been made much easier with the use of tech, allwoing for systems to be armed or disarmed remotely, and can send alerts to both emergency services and homeowners. This has been implemented via the use of automated smart locks, some of which support biometric authentication and temporary access codes, helpful for granting access to trusted individuals wihtout giving out physical keys.
